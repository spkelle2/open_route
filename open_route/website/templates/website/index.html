Welcome to Open-Route

Open-Route is an open source application for hosting vehicle routing services with an intuitive interface. Open-Route exists to prove that mathematical optimization is not just a means for organizations to save money, but that it can actually be a source of revenue when embedded into an application and offered as a service.

Explanation of the Vehicle Routing Model

The current model embedded in Open-Route describes a delivery network where all items being delivered are the exact size of the truck making the delivery. All items are identical and each site demands an integer amount of items. A positive demand of x represents that a site needs x items picked up. Demand of -x means the site needs x items dropped off.

Travel rate is the expected average rate of travel for trucks in the delivery network. Day length is the maximum amount of time someone driving a truck can work in a day. Work includes both the travel time between sites when making deliveries, but also the amount of time for handling, the unloading or loading of an item from the truck.

A special feature of this model is that it allows for the rescheduling of deliveries within a window of days. For example, if the window is 2 days, items to be picked up can be grabbed the day they are scheduled or the day after; items to be dropped off can be delivered the day they're demand or the day before. The model will try to even out the number of deliveries being made each day as much as possible such that the fewest number of drivers and trucks will be needed to satisfy the demands. To turn off this feature, set the window = 1.
